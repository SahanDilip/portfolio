NanoProcessor â€” 4-bit Educational CPU

A compact, hand-designed 4-bit processor implemented in VHDL and validated with testbenches and an FPGA (BASYS3). Designed to demonstrate datapath/control design, modular component integration, and hardware verification principles.

Key Features

  • Small Instruction Set: Implements 4 core instructions (MOVI, ADD, NEG, JZR) ideal for teaching control & datapath design.
  • Modular Component Design: Separate reusable modules for add/sub unit, adder, program counter, and multiplexers.
  • Register Bank: 4-bit register bank with read/write control for general-purpose registers used by the instruction set.
  • Program ROM & Decoder: ROM-based instruction storage with dedicated decoder for control signal generation.
  • Clocking & Debug: Built-in slow clock generation for step-by-step debugging and FPGA board synchronization.
  • Testbenches & Simulation: Extensive VHDL testbenches with XSIM/ModelSim for comprehensive functional verification.
  • FPGA Implementation: Synthesized with Xilinx Vivado and tested on BASYS3 development board.
  • Reports & Artifacts: Complete timing, utilization, and route reports with bitstream generation and TCL scripts.

Designed Components

  • 4-bit Add/Subtract Unit: ALU supporting add/subtract operations including NEG (two's complement).
  • 3-bit Adder: Used for program counter increments and address arithmetic operations.
  • Program Counter: 3-bit counter holding instruction addresses with conditional jump support.
  • Multiplexers: 2-way, 8-way multiplexers for datapath routing and register selection.
  • Instruction Decoder: Decodes opcodes and generates control signals for datapath elements.

Tech Stack

VHDL, Xilinx Vivado (synthesis & implementation), XSIM/ModelSim (simulation), BASYS3 FPGA, TCL scripts & testbenches
